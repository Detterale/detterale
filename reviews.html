My gear

From my experience, the Cort Flow OC Natural Satin has become my go-to for intimate and unplugged sessions. The guitar’s balanced tone makes it perfect for playing both fingerstyle and strumming. Whether I'm picking out intricate melodies or just playing rhythm, the smaller body of this acoustic guitar (likely concert-sized) is incredibly comfortable to hold, especially during long practice sessions.

The Yamaha Pacifica Standard Plus SPB RF is the guitar that I rely on when I need flexibility. Its HSS pickup configuration (humbucker at the bridge, single-coils in the middle and neck) provides an incredible range of tones. This makes it suitable for a wide variety of styles—from bluesy cleans to crunchy rock rhythms and searing leads.

The Harley Benton SC-500 WH is the guitar I turn to when I’m craving a more classic, vintage tone. With its Les Paul-inspired design, this guitar really shines when I’m playing heavier, riff-based music or anything that requires a lot of sustain and body.

The Cort C4 Deluxe Black is hands down my workhorse when it comes to bass playing. Whether I’m laying down funky basslines, grooving on rock tracks, or playing melodic bass for jazz and blues, this bass guitar has delivered consistently.
